\section{Technical Review Readiness}

This manuscript is ready for technical review in the following sense:
the pipeline now has a complete fresh live run under the natural response
protocol, executable validation checks, regenerated paper tables, and a run
bundle containing Frank, Karthic, Dasha, Judge, Zak, response, manifest, and
hash artifacts. The paper is not framed as a final external-validity claim. It
is framed as a methods and evidence paper that identifies exactly what
has been demonstrated and what remains before academic submission.

For a technical review team, the principal review questions are now concrete.
First, does Frank's source-to-packet artifact preserve the legally controlling
facts and avoid answer leakage in the generated question? Second, does Karthic's
rubric capture the relevant gates, defenses, counterarguments, source limits,
and variation sensitivity without double-counting? Third, do Dasha's 15
track-aware observed clusters reflect meaningful legal-reasoning differences or
merely over-split wording differences? Fourth, does the judge apply each rubric
row in a legally defensible way, and does adjudication resolve unstable panel
rows without masking disagreement? Fifth, are Zak triggers sufficiently
sensitive to low-confidence or mixed-cluster cases?

The answers to those questions determine the next research milestone. The
reported implementation satisfies the internal pipeline-readiness standard: a
source case can produce all major artifacts and pass the executable internal
gates in the method-readiness report. The current report marks all 11 internal
vision gates as met, including source provenance, Frank, Karthic, natural
response protocol, Dasha clustering, perturbation validation, judge scoring,
Zak escalation, run-bundle integrity, controlled scale regression, and live-run
preflight. The implementation therefore supports internal technical review as a
complete pre-expert-review methods artifact. It still does not establish the
stronger external publication-readiness standard, which requires held-out
source cases, expert review, target reliability thresholds across cases,
qualitative error analysis, and paper tables generated from frozen review
evidence.
