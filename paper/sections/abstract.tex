\begin{abstract}
We present a source-grounded pipeline for evaluating legal reasoning in large
language model outputs. The pipeline begins with a legal source case rather than
a hand-authored benchmark question. An LLM agent, Frank, extracts the legal
issue, controlling doctrine, source-grounded gold answer, and controlled
boundary variations. Karthic generates a fresh rubric from the locked Frank
packet. A roster of LLMs then answers the same generated question using the
natural question-only protocol. Dasha extracts normalized legal-reasoning
signatures from those answers and clusters them into interpretable centroids. A
rubric-guided LLM judge scores each centroid, projects centroid scores to all
responses in the cluster, and produces model rankings. Zak creates escalation
packets only when uncertainty, disagreement, or mixed clusters require further
review.

We report internal validation on a Statute-of-Frauds calibration case. The
evidence combines stage-level artifact assessment, a deterministic
question-perturbation smoke test, a fresh nine-response live model batch, and a
deterministic 500-response regression suite over eight Statute-of-Frauds
reasoning archetypes.
The live batch used \texttt{gpt-5.2}, \texttt{claude-sonnet-4-20250514}, and
\texttt{meta/meta-llama-3-70b-instruct}; it produced three Dasha reasoning
clusters, 30 row-level judge scores, projected member scores, and model
rankings. The controlled stress suite produced eight expected clusters with
cluster purity, completeness, and macro-F1 equal to 1.0. These results support
internal technical review of the pipeline as a reproducible research method:
Frank produces a source-grounded benchmark packet, Karthic produces a dynamic
rubric, Dasha separates observed legal-reasoning paths, Judge applies the rubric
row by row, and Zak records escalation state. They do not yet establish external
legal validity or final publication readiness: held-out cases, broader model
rosters, judge stability studies, and expert legal review remain required before
stronger academic claims.
\end{abstract}
