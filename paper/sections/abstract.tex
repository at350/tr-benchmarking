\begin{abstract}
We present a source-grounded pipeline for evaluating legal reasoning in large
language model outputs. The pipeline begins with a legal source case rather than
a hand-authored benchmark question. An LLM agent, Frank, extracts the legal
issue, controlling doctrine, source-grounded gold answer, and controlled
boundary variations. Karthic generates a fresh rubric from the locked Frank
packet. A roster of LLMs then answers the generated question in a controlled
format. Dasha clusters responses by normalized legal reasoning signatures,
reducing many answers into interpretable centroids. A rubric-guided LLM judge
scores those centroids, projects centroid scores to all responses in each
cluster, and produces model rankings. Zak creates escalation packets only when
uncertainty, disagreement, or mixed clusters require further review. We report
pre-expert-review internal validation on Statute of Frauds. The validation
combines a real nine-response natural model batch with a deterministic
500-response regression test over eight Statute-of-Frauds reasoning archetypes.
The current evidence
shows that the pipeline runs from source case to ranked model outputs, captures
provenance and hashes, preserves row-level judge rationales, projects centroid
scores, and exposes escalation state. This manuscript is complete as an
internal validation paper: it makes no claim of external expert-reviewed legal
validity.
\end{abstract}
