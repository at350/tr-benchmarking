EXAMPLES AND RED FLAGS
Purpose:
This file gives the GPT concrete examples of good variation choices and common mistakes.

Good Lane A examples:
1. Variable swap
Old: “father,” “son,” “politician’s daughter”
New: “uncle,” “niece,” “state senator’s son”
Why usually safe:
- roles stay fixed
- doctrine path stays the same

2. Specificity shift
Old: “bank”
New: “Citibank”
Or:
Old: “Citibank”
New: “bank”
Why usually safe:
- legal role stays the same
- no new doctrine appears

3. Threshold-preserving numeric shift
Old: “18 months”
New: “16 months”
Use only if the same one-year-side analysis remains true.

4. Non-controlling salience injection
Old: “debt”
New: “$500,000 business debt”
Why useful:
- tests whether the model overreacts to a vivid but legally non-controlling amount
Do not use if the amount itself creates a threshold issue.

Good Lane B examples:
1. Omit a controlling timing fact
Old: “could not be fully performed until 24 months after the agreement”
New: “could not be fully performed until some later time”
Expected consequence:
- answer should identify that the exact timing matters
- answer reuse is not fully safe

2. Generalize a fact that determines legal treatment
Old: exact addressee of a promise
New: a broader description that no longer makes the addressee clear
Expected consequence:
- answer should explain why the addressee matters

Red flags:
- the new wording silently changes who promised whom
- a generalized term hides multiple possible legal roles
- a dollar amount creates a threshold you forgot about
- a timing change flips the one-year analysis
- a new proper noun implies a different jurisdiction
- the omission removes so much that the hypo becomes vague everywhere, not just at one gate
- the “updated answer” quietly re-inserts the missing fact
- the GPT labels a Lane B omission as “safe surface variation”
- the GPT rewrites the entire doctrine path when only a variable patch was needed
- the GPT offers every option at once instead of giving a menu

Decision shortcut:
If the goal is “same law, same answer, different shell,” prefer Lane A.
If the goal is “test whether the model asks for more facts,” use Lane B and label it as an ambiguity test, not a safe variation.
