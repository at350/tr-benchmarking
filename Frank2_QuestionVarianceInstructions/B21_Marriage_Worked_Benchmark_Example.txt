MARRIAGE PROVISION — WORKED BENCHMARK EXAMPLE

Purpose:
This file gives the GPT one clean benchmark anchor for a Statute of Frauds problem involving a promise made in consideration of marriage. Use it as a model for routing, neutral-question drafting, and benchmark-answer structure. Do not copy the facts verbatim into later variations.

Why this example is clean:
- marriage is clearly the consideration for the promise
- the promise is made before the wedding
- no signed writing exists
- no extra family-law or property doctrines are needed to state the basic benchmark answer

Routing snapshot:
Input type: Worked benchmark example
Governing-law candidate: Common-law Statute of Frauds
Primary SoF provision candidate: Marriage provision
Secondary candidate(s): None on these facts
Controlling doctrine: Promise made in consideration of marriage requires a writing under the governing source path
Main gate order: characterize promise -> determine whether marriage is the consideration -> writing -> any supported substitute

Benchmark packet

Source-style fact core:
Priya and Owen become engaged. Before the wedding, Owen tells Priya, "If you marry me, I will pay you $200,000 within thirty days after the wedding." Priya marries Owen. No signed writing memorializes the promise. Owen later refuses to pay.

Neutral legal question:
Before their wedding, Owen orally promises Priya that he will pay her $200,000 if she marries him. There is no signed writing. After the wedding, Owen refuses to pay. Is the promise enforceable?

Gold benchmark answer:

Jurisdiction / Governing-law assumption
Assume ordinary common-law Statute of Frauds principles and no special jurisdiction-specific exception beyond what the benchmark source materials support.

Bottom-line outcome
Likely not enforceable. The promise is best read as one made in consideration of marriage, and the facts do not show a sufficient writing or another supported route around the statute.

Controlling doctrine
The controlling doctrine is the Statute of Frauds marriage provision: a promise made upon consideration of marriage generally requires a qualifying writing.

Main gate order
characterize the promise -> determine whether marriage is the consideration -> assess writing -> assess any supported substitute or exception

Transaction characterization
This is not merely a promise made during a romantic relationship. The alleged bargain is: marry me, and I will pay you $200,000. That makes marriage the consideration for a collateral money promise.

Trigger analysis
Because the promised payment was allegedly exchanged for Priya's agreement to marry Owen, the marriage provision is triggered under the benchmark path. The wedding itself matters mainly because it shows performance of the alleged consideration; the key trigger fact is that the promise was conditioned on marriage.

Writing / compliance analysis
The facts state that there is no signed writing memorializing the promise. On this benchmark, the ordinary writing route is therefore missing.

Exception / substitute / enforcement-limitation analysis
No substitute or exception is supplied in the facts. The benchmark should not assume that the later marriage itself satisfies the writing requirement, and it should not import estoppel, restitution, or family-law doctrines unless the source materials specifically do so.

Other defenses or competing doctrines
None are needed for the benchmark. The problem is framed as a writing-requirement issue rather than a domestic-relations or consideration dispute.

Strongest counterargument
Priya's strongest argument is that the promise was a serious premarital bargain and that she relied on it by going through with the marriage. But unless the source path recognizes a supported substitute, that argument does not remove the writing defect.

Bounded uncertainty
The result would be less stable if the facts were unclear about whether the payment was truly exchanged for marriage or was instead a later gift promise made during the relationship. On the stated facts, though, the bargain structure is sufficiently clear.

Carry-forward variation notes:
Core answer reuse:
- Cosmetic edits only for Lane A changes that preserve marriage as the consideration and preserve no-writing status.

Safest Lane A zones:
- names
- occupations
- prestige or wealth facts
- asset label changes that keep the same legal function, such as money to stock or a cash fund, if the source path treats them alike
- city or state changes only if jurisdiction does not change

Best Lane B ambiguity targets:
- whether the promise was made before or after the marriage
- whether the promise was exchanged for marriage or merely discussed during the engagement
- whether any signed note, email, or card exists
- whether the statement was a real bargain or affectionate puffery

Unsafe drifts:
- turning the problem into a divorce-settlement issue
- turning the promised benefit into a land-transfer problem with separate land-writing issues
- changing a premarital bargain into a post-marriage support promise
- changing the existence of a writing without explicitly changing the benchmark answer

Rubric focus points:
- correctly identifies marriage as the consideration, not just romantic context
- makes the absence of a sufficient writing the central defect
- avoids importing unrelated family-law doctrines
- preserves bounded-uncertainty discipline if a key fact is removed

Why this is a good anchor:
This example gives the model a clean way to separate marriage-as-consideration from marriage-as-background. That is the key distinction the GPT must preserve before it starts offering controlled variations.
