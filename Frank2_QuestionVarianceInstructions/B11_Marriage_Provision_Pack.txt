STATUTE OF FRAUDS PACK — MARRIAGE PROVISION

Purpose:
This pack helps the GPT handle promises made in consideration of marriage without overextending the marriage provision to every promise made by romantic partners, engaged couples, or spouses.

Scope statement:
Use this pack only when the likely controlling issue is whether marriage itself is the bargained-for consideration for a promise and therefore triggers a writing requirement under the governing source path.
Do not use this pack just because the facts mention dating, engagement, marriage, divorce, cohabitation, or family relationships.

Baseline source-of-truth rule:
The benchmark answer and source materials control.
This pack is a routing-and-variation guide, not a state-by-state family-law treatise.
Do not import domestic-relations doctrines, equitable-distribution principles, or support obligations unless the source materials actually require them.

Use this pack when:
- a party allegedly promised money, property, a transfer, a settlement, or another benefit in exchange for marrying
- the problem asks whether a premarital promise is enforceable without a writing
- the key dispute is whether marriage is the consideration or merely the factual setting

Do not use this pack when:
- the promise was merely made while the parties were romantically involved
- the promise is an ordinary post-marriage contract unless the source materials still treat marriage as the consideration
- the controlling issue is family law, property division, or another doctrine outside the benchmark

Default gate order:
1. characterize the promise
2. determine whether marriage is the consideration or merely background context
3. determine whether the source path requires a signed writing and whether one exists
4. evaluate only those substitute or enforcement theories actually supported by the source materials
5. identify bounded uncertainty if the consideration structure or writing status is unclear

Anchor facts that must stay fixed in Lane A:
- whether marriage is the bargain or merely the backdrop
- whether the promise was made before marriage, after marriage, or during a romantic relationship without marriage as consideration
- whether any writing or signed memorandum exists
- who is promising what to whom if that affects the consideration structure
- whether the benchmark already treats a particular exception as available or unavailable

Lane A safe variation zones:
1. party-shell swaps
Examples:
- names
- occupations
- social status labels
- city or state only if jurisdiction does not change

2. asset-description swaps
Examples:
- “house” -> “condominium”
- “stocks” -> “investment account”
- “family cabin” -> “vacation property”
Use only if the asset plays the same legal function and does not import a new property doctrine.

3. salience injections that should not matter
Examples:
- high-value transfer
- prestigious job title
- well-known employer
Use only if the extra detail does not create a new defense or a different doctrinal frame.

Lane B ambiguity targets:
1. consideration ambiguity
Old:
“I will transfer Blackacre to you if you marry me.”
New:
“I will transfer the property to you because of our relationship.”
Why this matters:
The new wording may no longer clearly show that marriage is the consideration.

2. timing ambiguity
Old:
“One week before the wedding, A promised to give B an investment account if B married A.”
New:
“At some point before or around the marriage, A made the promise.”
Why this matters:
The omission may blur whether the promise was truly part of the marriage bargain.

3. writing-status ambiguity
Old:
“The promise was never put in writing.”
New:
“The parties discussed the promise informally.”
Why this matters:
The answer should now identify that the existence and sufficiency of a writing matters.

Unsafe variation patterns:
- turning a premarital exchange-for-marriage promise into a general promise between spouses
- changing who provided the consideration in a way that shifts the doctrine
- silently changing the promise from marriage-related consideration to a property-distribution agreement
- adding facts that trigger family-law doctrines not present in the benchmark
- removing so much that the problem no longer reveals whether the marriage provision is even in play

Recommended default menu seeds for B02:
Option 1 — Lane A / Variable swap / Relationship-shell neutralization
What changes:
Names, occupations, and asset labels only.
Expected answer reuse:
Cosmetic edits only.
Main red flag:
Do not change whether marriage is the bargain.

Option 2 — Lane A / Non-controlling salience injection
What changes:
Adds vivid but non-doctrinal detail about the promised asset or parties.
Expected answer reuse:
Reuse as-is or Cosmetic edits only.
Main red flag:
Do not create a new property or domestic-relations issue.

Option 3 — Lane B / Marriage-consideration ambiguity
What changes:
Generalizes the language linking the promise to marriage.
Expected answer reuse:
Ambiguity rewrite required.
Main red flag:
Do not hide multiple unrelated doctrines at once.

Worked micro examples:
Safe Lane A:
Old:
“A promised B a vacation home if B married A. There was no writing.”
New:
“A software founder promised B an investment condo if B married A. There was no writing.”
Why usually safe:
The promise still appears to exchange a benefit for marriage and the writing status remains unchanged.

Safe Lane B:
Old:
“Before the wedding, A promised to transfer a brokerage account to B if B married A.”
New:
“Before the relationship formally changed, A promised to transfer a brokerage account to B.”
Why this works:
The model should now identify that it is unclear whether marriage itself was the consideration.

Unsafe:
Old:
“Before the wedding, A promised B land if B married A.”
New:
“Years into the marriage, A said B could have the land.”
Why unsafe:
The doctrinal trigger may change from a marriage-consideration promise to an ordinary intra-marital promise or a different doctrine entirely.

Answer-shape emphasis:
Jurisdiction / Governing-law assumption:
Keep this brief unless the source materials make a jurisdiction-specific writing or exception rule important.

Bottom-line outcome:
State whether the promise likely falls within the marriage provision and whether the absence or presence of a writing matters.

Controlling doctrine:
Say plainly whether the key gate is “marriage as consideration” versus “marriage merely as background.”

Transaction characterization:
Describe the promise and why the relationship context either does or does not trigger the marriage provision.

Trigger analysis:
Focus on the bargain structure, not the romance narrative.

Writing / compliance analysis:
Explain whether the relevant writing exists and whether the benchmark path treats it as sufficient.

Exception / substitute analysis:
Stay narrow.
Only include substitute or equitable theories if the benchmark answer already does so.

Strongest counterargument:
Usually the strongest response-side argument is that the promise was not actually made in consideration of marriage, or that an adequate writing exists.

Bounded uncertainty:
If the facts do not clearly show that marriage was the consideration, say exactly that.

Rubric focus points:
A strong answer should:
- identify that romantic context alone does not trigger the marriage provision
- state whether marriage is the consideration
- keep the writing analysis tied to the correct trigger
- avoid importing unrelated family-law doctrines
- acknowledge bounded uncertainty when the bargain structure is missing or blurred

Main red flags:
- “engaged” or “married” is treated as automatically triggering the provision
- the answer narrates relationship facts without isolating the consideration question
- the updated answer quietly assumes a writing after a Lane B omission removed that fact
- the variation changes pre-marital to post-marital timing without labeling that doctrinal shift
- the model inserts domestic-relations terminology that the benchmark never used

Status rule:
Mark the package Ready only if the promise still clearly sits in the same marriage-provision lane, or if a Lane B ambiguity test clearly isolates the missing control fact.
Otherwise mark Needs targeted revision or Unsafe.
