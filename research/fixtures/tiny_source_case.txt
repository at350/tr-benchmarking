Illinois appellate source excerpt for calibration.

A member of a fraternal benefit association told his fiancee before their
wedding: "If you marry me, I will change my benefit certificate so that you
are the beneficiary." She agreed, they married, and he later obtained a new
certificate naming her as beneficiary. The wife kept possession of that
certificate. About a year later, the marriage deteriorated. The member told
the association that he could not produce the certificate because his wife had
it. Under the association rules, he obtained a replacement certificate naming
his children and sister as beneficiaries instead. After the member died, the
wife and the later-named beneficiaries each claimed the death benefit.

The source focuses on whether the premarital promise and later certificate
created enforceable rights for the wife under the marriage provision of the
Statute of Frauds and related association rules.
