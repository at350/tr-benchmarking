In New York, Apex drafted a software-license agreement with a remedy covenant stating that "the buyer may pursue all remedies available at law or in equity for uncured service failures." A later schedule says service credits are "the exclusive operational remedy" for routine outages. The buyer alleges a severe uncured outage and seeks damages. The seller argues the service-credit schedule limits all remedies. The buyer argues the plain meaning of the remedy covenant preserves damages for severe uncured failures, or at least that any ambiguity should be construed against Apex as drafter.
